Optimization

Optimization consists in minimizing or maximizing an objective function subject to restrictions imposed by the model. Many times, the objective function is simply the cost of production of an item and the restrictions follow the conditions imposed by the manufacturing process, availability of supplies and several other factors.

Our cut pattern optimizer.

To obtain efficient cuts in MDF/MDP boards, it is necessary to generate many different cut patterns that together minimize the total amount of material used.

In order to compute the solution, many algorithms are used. There are algorithms specialized in generating efficient cut patterns and other algorithms, such as the Simplex Method and Genetic Programming, that are used to compose of a solution given the individual patterns that were generated.

Detection of fraudulent accounts

Using a huge database where a fraction of the accounts was flagged as fraudulent (either manually or through some other methods), we train a Random Forests model for the automatic classification of suspicious accounts.

This model consists of a collection of decision trees, each of them analyzing a small subset of the variables and outputting a vote ('suspicious account' or 'normal account'). The decision of the forest depends on the votes of its constituent trees (e.g., 2/3 of votes may be required to classify an account as suspicious).

The forest is constructed by algorithms that analyze a sample of the data with the pre-assigned classification. The quality of the resulting forest is verified using cross validation, namely, the classifier is tested against part of the sample which was not used to train it.

African Origins

We implemented the approximate name search in the African-Origins website. The African names were registered inconsistenly by speakers of different languages, and most of the names in the records are somewhat different from the modern spelling. We used a trie data structure to store the name index and developed an algorithm that quickly calculates a distance based on spelling changes to order names by proximity to the searched term.

The whole solution was designed with high performance in mind, and on average, it takes less than 30 milliseconds to find the 100 names closest to the searched term, in a database of almost 100,000 records (the solution was implemented as a web-service programmed in Java).



Noise reduction in images
It is possible to drastically reduce certain types of noise by using morphological characteristics of the images.

Automatic design of morphological operators

In this scientific work, joint with the Computational Vision and the Continuous Optimization groups at the University of São Paulo, we developed a theoretical basis and software implementation of the automatic design of morphological operators for the reduction of image noise based on statistical training.

Our article was published in the prestigious IEEE Transactions on Image Processing, vol. 16, issue 2, pg. 453-462 (2007). The source code as well as the cross validation tests are available here.
